ISO 27001 Annex A Overview
Annex A contains security controls grouped into domains such as access control, cryptography, operations security, and incident management. These controls help organizations manage risks systematically.

A.5 Information Security Policies
Organizations must define and maintain information security policies approved by management and communicated internally.

A.6 Organization of Information Security
Defines roles and responsibilities for information security governance and segregation of duties.

A.7 Human Resource Security
Ensures employees understand security responsibilities before, during, and after employment.

A.8 Asset Management
Organizations must identify assets, assign ownership, and define acceptable use policies.

A.9 Access Control
Access must be restricted based on business needs and least privilege principles.

A.9.2.3 Management of Privileged Access Rights
Privileged accounts must be tightly controlled, monitored, and reviewed periodically.

A.10 Cryptography
Use encryption to protect confidentiality and integrity of sensitive data.

A.12 Operations Security
Logging, monitoring, and change management must be implemented to protect operations.

A.12.4 Logging and Monitoring
Event logs must be produced, protected, and regularly reviewed.

A.13 Communications Security
Networks must be secured and data protected during transmission.

A.16 Incident Management
Organizations must have incident response procedures and reporting mechanisms.

A.18 Compliance
Ensure adherence to legal, regulatory, and contractual requirements.
